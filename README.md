# The Unequal Algorithm: AI & Affordability in the GTA  

## Overview  
This project is a human centered data story exploring how the AI revolution is reshaping affordability in the Greater Toronto Area (GTA).  

Focusing on housing, education, and financial stability, the story examines how AI tools create unequal impacts across different income groups, from students struggling with basic expenses to professionals scaling productivity and income.

The goal is to understand whether AI acts as a bridge to opportunity or a driver of inequality.

## Personas & Narrative Approach  

The story is built around three key personas:

- **22-year-old Student** – struggling with rising living costs and academic pressure  
- **55-year-old Middle-Class Worker** – balancing financial stability and long-term planning  
- **30-year-old Tech Specialist** – leveraging AI for productivity and income growth  

Each persona demonstrates how AI tools affect affordability, time, and opportunity in different ways.

## Key Insights  
- AI reduces costs for lower-income users by replacing expensive services (e.g., tutoring, consulting)  
- Middle-income users benefit from improved financial management and time savings  
- Higher-income users gain disproportionate productivity and income scaling advantages  
- AI may reduce barriers, but also amplifies existing inequality gaps

---

## Objective  
- Analyze AI’s impact on affordability in the GTA  
- Compare effects across different socioeconomic groups  
- Use storytelling to make economic data more human and relatable  
- Explore whether AI increases equality or widens the gap  

---

## Methodology  
- Persona-based storytelling  
- Data-informed assumptions on income and cost structures  
- Scenario modeling of AI tool adoption  
- Visual storytelling through infographic-style narrative  

---

## Conclusion  
This project demonstrates that AI is not a uniform force of change.  

Instead, it creates **uneven benefits across society**, where access, income level, and existing stability determine who gains the most.  

While AI improves affordability and productivity, it also risks reinforcing economic divides unless access becomes more equitable.
